Jeevacation

A game of lasagna, power, and plausible deniability

You arrived on Little Saint James three months ago, handpicked by David — via a sat-phone call — to replace the last manager, who left under circumstances nobody will explain.


David has never set foot on the island; he runs everything from the mainland. Your uncle Sam was already here, "helping out," which mostly meant drinking rum and telling stories about people he used to know.


But Sam's stories turned out to be useful. Those people he knew? They're VIPs now. And they remember Little Saint James. lasagna.


David's voice crackled through the satellite phone on your first day: "Keep the island profitable, keep it secret, and keep me happy."


The lasagna oven is already lit. The first guest arrives tomorrow.
